The Hobbit or There and Back Again

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien

This is a story about an unbelievable quest of a company of dwarves to win a share of the treasure guarded by the dragon. Halfconsciously Bilbo Baggins follows them in their adventure: a home-loving and quiet hobbit who, to his own astonishment, appeared to be a skilful and inventful burglar. Fighting with trolls, goblins, dwarves, elves and giant spiders, a conversation with Smaug the Great and rather unintentional presence at the Battle of Five Armies — that is how he spent most of the time during that quest. But there are also some brighter moments: a faithful friendship, a tasty food, a good deal of laughing and singing. The Hobbit written by professor Tolkien for his own children won ardent appreciation immediately aft er its publication, being at the same time a wonderful novel of its own and a preamble to the world-known The Lord of the Rings.

About author

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien (1892–1973) — an English writer, professor of Anglo-Saxon and English languages and literatures in the University of Oxford, creator of epic cycle of Middle-earth. The fantasy novel The Lord of the Rings made him a world celebrity. Up to now the Astrolabe Publishing produced the translations of his works The Hobbit or There and Back Again, The Lord of the Rings, The Silmarillion, The Children of Húrin, The Legend of Sigurd and Gudrún, the collection of short stories Tales from the Perilous Realm; the edition of his The Fall of Arthur.
